# Chapter 1 — *Everything Smells Like Cardboard and New Beginnings*

> **Status:** ✅ Complete.
> **Setting:** Move-in day, Fall, Year 1. Sumeru dormitory wing.

---

## Prose

The first thing Aether noticed about Teyvat University was the noise. Not bad noise,
exactly — just *a lot* of it. The whole campus hummed like a beehive that had been
gently kicked. He stood at the foot of the Sumeru dormitory steps with two duffel bags
and a box, trying to look like someone who knew where he was going. He did not.

Lumine, his twin, breezed ahead — already greeting people by name, balancing a milk
crate like she'd done it her whole life. She teased him about his "lost-puppy face,"
pointed him toward Room 304, and announced she'd be three floors up and intended to
abandon him "logistically, immediately" to go find the dining hall.

**Room 304** had his name hand-lettered in green marker with a little doodled star. Two
beds, two desks, one window over the vine-covered courtyard, institutional beige. His
roommate hadn't arrived yet. He'd barely started unpacking when the visitors began.

**Alhaitham** (Room 305, shared wall) knocked, introduced himself flatly, warned that
the radiator "sounds like a dying whale at 2am," noticed Aether reads, and left.

**Collei** appeared next — anxious, clutching a clipboard, delivering welcome packets
(map, dining hours, an expired convenience-store coupon). She and Aether bonded over
both being lost, undecided first-years. She mentioned her TA.

**Cyno**, the third-floor RA, arrived booming for Collei about overdue packets — "I'm
not upset. I'm *invested.*" — welcomed Aether, plugged the Friday floor meeting (snacks),
and deadpanned a pun about "the *bear* necessities" before sweeping out.

Alone again, Aether took a breath amid the boxes. Three people in one afternoon already
felt like a foothold. His phone buzzed — Lumine, at the dining hall, mentioning a busker
playing a tiny guitar outside the library, telling him to come find her "or don't, ur an
adult, allegedly." The first real decision of his college life, and somehow it felt
enormous.

**[Choice D]** Aether seized the move-in energy and wandered the floor. In **306** he
met **Kaveh**, a dramatic blond architecture student mid-crisis over his room's "dead"
north-facing light — who lit up at a sincere compliment to his drawings, declared they'd
be friends, and revealed he's Alhaitham's long-suffering roommate. Further down the hall
he found **Layla**, a sleep-deprived STEM floormate lost on the way to room 312; Aether
helped her orient and they bonded over neither having it figured out.

**[Choices F → A]** Back at 304, Aether finished unpacking (the room became *a* room),
then texted Lumine and braved the chaotic-but-warm dining hall. Over stolen fries and a
hard-won soft serve, the twins reconnected; Lumine quietly checked he was okay, and he
realized he meant it when he said he was. A busker with a tiny guitar played by the
doors. He walked back to a hushed hallway, turned off the lamp — and at 2 a.m., exactly
as promised, the radiator made a sound like a dying whale, and he fell asleep laughing.
